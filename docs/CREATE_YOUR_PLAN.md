# Create Your Plan Without the App

Two paths to building a family disaster plan without using the Rally Point web app. Both produce the same content as the app — you're just filling in the blanks manually or with AI assistance instead of clicking through a wizard.

---

Download and print the template below. Fill it in with a pen. No technology required. Works during a power outage.

## Path B: AI-Assisted Plan Generation

Copy the prompt below and paste it into Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI assistant. The AI will interview you with questions and produce a completed plan document you can print.

---

### AI INTERVIEW PROMPT

```
You are helping me create a family disaster response plan. Please interview me 
to gather the information needed, then produce a complete formatted plan I can 
print and use.

Interview me in this order — ask one section at a time, wait for my answers, 
then move to the next:

1. FAMILY UNITS: Ask about each household in my plan (one at a time):
   - Names of people in the household
   - Ages (especially children and elderly)
   - Home address
   - Home type (house/apartment/mobile home)
   - Basement? Safe room?
   - Vehicles: type, fuel (gas/electric/hybrid), approximate range, seats
   - Pets: name, type, has a carrier?
   - Any medical needs or medications?

2. RALLY POINTS: Ask:
   - Which households are geographically close to each other?
   - Whose house would be the best "hub" for each geographic cluster?
   - What resources are at each hub? (generator, stored water/food, shelter 
     capacity, NOAA radio, FRS radios)
   - Is there a full-family convergence hub if an emergency extends 3+ days?
   - Who is the out-of-state coordinator? (Name, phone, relationship)

3. COMMUNICATION: Ask:
   - How many FRS walkie-talkies do they have total?
   - What channels should each household use?
   - Preferred check-in times (default: 8am, 12pm, 6pm)?
   - Any Meshtastic or ham radio equipment?
   - 2-3 trusted neighbor contacts?

4. DEPARTURE SIGNAL: Ask:
   - Where will each household leave a note when they evacuate?
   - What is their visual marker (object placed to signal they've left)?

5. PASSPHRASE: Ask:
   - Challenge word and response phrase
   - Physical token description (optional)
   - Backup phrase

6. AWAY-FROM-HOME: Ask:
   - Where does each adult typically work?
   - Do any children go to school? Where?
   - Does anyone travel regularly for work?

7. EVACUATION ROUTES: Ask:
   - What are the major highways to AVOID in an evacuation?
   - What county roads or alternate routes should each unit take?

After gathering all this information, produce a complete formatted Family 
Disaster Response Plan that includes:
- Cover page with plan name and date
- Communication plan with contact tree
- Rally point details with resource inventories and "bring this" lists
- Departure signal cards (one per household)
- Passphrase reference card
- Away-from-home protocols per person
- Evacuation routes
- Go-bag checklist (personalized for the family's composition)
- A printable wallet card template

Format it clearly so I can print it and organize it in a binder.
```

---

Paste this prompt, answer the questions, and the AI will generate your complete plan. Copy the output into a Word document or Google Doc, format it, and print.
